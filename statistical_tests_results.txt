 Statistical Validation: Results & Findings

Date: 2026-04-17
Dataset: master_data.csv (March-April 2026 window)

 1. Hypothesis 5: T-Test vs. Z-Proxy (Tech vs. Defensive Returns)
Goal: Validate if the return difference between Tech and Defensive archetypes is statistically significant.

   Population Z-Proxy (Wilcoxon Rank-Sum): 
       Statistic: 1.3261
       P-value: 0.1848
   Sampling Analysis (Bootstrapped T-stat, n=5):
       The sampling distribution shows that with small sample sizes, the results often fluctuate across the alpha=0.05 threshold.
   Finding: The p-value of 0.1848 is greater than 0.05. We cannot reject the null hypothesis. While the Tech portfolio shows a trend of higher volatility/returns in the EDA, the difference is not statistically significant in this narrow 18-day window.

 2. Hypothesis 6: One-Way ANOVA (Cross-Portfolio Comparison)
Goal: Check if 'Portfolio Archetype' significantly impacts returns across all groups.

   F-Statistic: 0.7517
   P-value: 0.5254
   Finding: The high p-value (0.5254) suggests that the mean returns across Tech, Safe, Defensive, and Geo-Sensitive portfolios are not statistically distinct during this specific period. The sector archetypes behave similarly in terms of raw returns.

 3. Hypothesis 4: Chi-Square for Independence (News Risk vs. Market)
Goal: Test if "High Risk News" and "Negative Market Directions" are coupled.

   Contingency Table:
       Low Risk News / Positive Market: 8 days
       Low Risk News / Negative Market: 1 day
       High Risk News / Positive Market: 5 days
       High Risk News / Negative Market: 4 days
   Chi-Square Statistic: 1.1077
   P-value: 0.2926
   Finding: At p=0.29, we do not find a strong statistical dependency between Geopolitical risk sentiment and immediate market direction for this sample. 

 Final Conclusion
While the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) showed clear visual trends (e.g., Tech sensitivity and Geopolitical correlation), the Statistical Validation indicates that with the current sample size (n=18), these trends have not yet reached the threshold of statistical significance.

Recommendation: Increase the observation window (more historical data) to confirm if these signals persist and strengthen to provide better predictive footing for forecasting models.
