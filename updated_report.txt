# Abstract
This project builds a high-fidelity quantitative research and tactical advisory platform that quantifies how news-driven shocks across geopolitics, technology, and finance impact diversified stock portfolios. The system employs a Dual-Transformer NLP architecture (FinBERT for financial sentiment; RoBERTa for technology-domain nuance) to score 65,248 articles, aligns the resulting signals with daily market prices, and trains a six-model machine learning ensemble. Geopolitical sentiment emerges as the dominant predictor of short-term market movements. Results are exposed through a full-stack React + FastAPI dashboard featuring interactive risk visualisations and a GenAI advisory layer powered by Groq (Llama-3.1-70B) that generates natural-language justifications for every Buy/Reduce signal.

# 1. Introduction
Modern financial risk management requires moving beyond backward-looking price data. This project builds an end-to-end pipeline that captures high-velocity alternative data (news) to predict and justify portfolio risk adjustments, delivering a high-fidelity quantitative research and tactical advisory platform for institutional-grade portfolio governance.

## Objectives
1. Collect and clean news articles from multiple domains (geopolitics, technology, finance) and market data for a set of representative portfolios.
2. Generate sentiment scores using Dual Transformer NLP Architecture.
3. Engineer features that capture temporal relationships (lags, rolling volatility, sentiment velocity).
4. Test a series of hypotheses about the relationship between news sentiment and market behaviour.
5. Build and evaluate predictive models for shock detection, recovery time, risk scoring, and cross-domain lag effects.
6. Deploy an interactive dashboard for real-time monitoring.

# 2. Data Collection
## 2.1 Market Data
* Source: yfinance API.
* Assets: Individual stocks representing four portfolio archetypes (Tech, Geopolitical, Balanced, Conservative), plus safe-haven assets (Gold – GLD, Bonds – TLT) and the VIX index.
* Period: Initially March–April 2026; later extended to 2021–2026 for robustness.

## 2.2 News Data
* Sources: TechCrunch (RSS), Hacker News (Algolia API), Economic Times, and Moneycontrol. Reuters, BBC, Livemint, Reddit, and the Wayback Machine were deliberately excluded to ensure data integrity and avoid rate-limiting fragility.
* Scraping Scripts: `scrape_news.py`, `fetch_prices.py`, `incremental_updater.py` located in the `scrapers/` directory.
* Challenges: GDELT data formats were found to be non-ideal for fine-grained sentiment. Free-tier API restrictions capped the back-testing window at 64 months (2021–2026). Rate-limiting was mitigated by request throttling and domain-specific pagination.

# 3. Data Pre-processing
1. **Cleaning:** Removal of null rows, duplicate headlines, and non-informative text. Headlines were normalised (lower-casing, punctuation removal).
2. **Sentiment Scoring:** A Dual-Transformer architecture was employed — FinBERT for financial-domain sentiment and RoBERTa for technology-domain nuance. Together they scored 65,248 articles across three classes (Positive, Neutral, Negative) with high class separation.
3. **Temporal Alignment:** News is published seven days a week while markets trade five days. Weekend articles were shifted to Monday's session; holidays were forward-filled.
4. **Feature Engineering:** Log returns and 5-day / 20-day rolling volatility Z-scores. Sentiment velocity (day-to-day change). Lagged sentiment features (Lag-1 to Lag-7). Outlier clipping at ±3σ. Final master dataset (`master_data.csv`) contains 1,330 rows and 203 columns.

## 3.1 Cleaned Datasets & Scripts
The cleaned data files generated by the pipeline are:

3. `data/processed/ml_features.csv` – feature matrix used for model training.

**Key scripts used:**

# 4. Exploratory Data Analysis & Hypothesis Testing
The following table summarises the six hypotheses tested and their outcomes. A key headline finding from EDA is that geopolitical sentiment has 7× more impact on broad market (SPY) movements than finance-only news, and tech portfolios experience a 16% volatility spike on shock days.

| Hypothesis | Description | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **H1** | Geopolitical news drives market direction. | **Supported** – correlation 0.3427 (largest among domains). |
| **H2** | Tech portfolios are more volatile during negative news spikes. | **Supported** – 16% volatility increase on shock days. |
| **H3** | Safe-haven assets rise when geopolitical risk is high. | **Supported** – correlation 0.1467. |
| **H4** | Market absorbs shock immediately (no lag). | **Supported** – Lag-0 correlation –0.2383, Lag-1 weaker. |
| **H5** | Geopolitical sentiment is the strongest predictor of returns. | **Supported** – dominates over financial and tech sentiment. |
| **H6** | News shock leads to measurable lag in other domains. | **Partially Supported** – Granger tests show 3–5 day lag from geopolitics to tech. |

**Statistical validation was completely overhauled from static tests (Wilcoxon/Chi-Square) to rigorous time-series econometrics over a 5-year longitudinal dataset (2021–2026). The following core tests ensure robust statistical power:**

1. **Stationarity Suite (ADF & KPSS)**
To prevent spurious correlations, Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) and Kwiatkowski-Phillips-Schmidt-Shin (KPSS) tests were run. Results confirmed the daily return streams and Z-scored sentiment signals are stationary, rendering them safe for causality testing.

2. **Granger Causality (Proof of Lead-Lag)**
Applied to test causality from sentiment to returns at lags 1-3 days. Analysis uncovered significant causality (p < 0.05) proving that geopolitical sentiment actively leads technology asset volatility by up to 5 days.

3. **Johansen Cointegration**
Employed to prove that specific assets and news domains share a long-term equilibrium. Trace statistics confirmed cointegration between Geopolitical portfolio returns and Geopolitical sentiment.

4. **Multi-Regime ANOVA (Yearly Consistency)**
Evaluates the F-Statistic across the years 2021 through 2026 to ensure that the news-sensitivity differences between Archetypes (Tech vs Conservative) are a structural market feature rather than an anomaly of a specific year.

5. **ARIMAX Likelihood Ratio Test (LRT)**
Used to compare an ARIMA(1,0,1) baseline against an ARIMAX model featuring exogenous sentiment. Chi-square distribution tests on log-likelihood metrics confirmed that integrating sentiment reduces forecast error and improves predictability at a 95% confidence interval.

This 5-year longitudinal testing effectively mitigates the lack of statistical power in early iterations, confirming the observed hypotheses mathematically.

# 5. Machine Learning Models
The pipeline consists of six models, each addressing a specific research question. The table below provides a high-level overview, followed by detailed descriptions of each model.

| ID | Model | Goal | Method | Performance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **M1** | Shock Classifier | Detect volatility shocks | Random Forest (adaptive 95th-percentile thresholds) | F1 = 0.4187, AUC-ROC = 0.8560 |
| **M2** | Recovery Predictor | Estimate recovery days post-shock | Gradient Boosting Regressor (258 shock events) | Median MAE = 2.15 days |
| **M3** | Portfolio Risk Scorer | Daily risk score (0–100) with confidence band | Ridge Regression (sentiment-only features) | R² = 0.05–0.39; band width ≈ 0.003–0.006 |
| **M4** | Cross-Domain Lag Predictor | Identify lagged news-volatility relationships | ADF → CCF → Granger causality | Significant causality at lags 3, 4, 5 (p < 0.05) |
| **M5** | Sentiment-Volatility Regression | Quantify sentiment's effect on volatility | Ridge Regression with domain coefficients | Geopolitical: R² = 0.7321 |
| **M6** | Portfolio Clustering | Group portfolios by news sensitivity | K-Means (k = 2, silhouette-optimised) | All archetypes → single geopolitical-sensitive cluster |

## 5.1 M1 – Shock Classifier
* **Goal:** Detect whether a portfolio experiences a volatility shock on a given day.
* **Method:** Random Forest with per-stock adaptive 95th-percentile thresholds.
* **Performance:** Calibrated F1-Score = 0.4187, AUC-ROC = 0.8560.

## 5.2 M2 – Recovery Predictor
* **Goal:** Estimate the number of days required for volatility to return to baseline after a shock.
* **Method:** Gradient Boosting Regressor trained on pooled shock events (258 total).
* **Performance:** Median MAE = 2.15 days.

## 5.3 M3 – Portfolio Risk Scorer
* **Goal:** Produce a daily risk score (0–100) with a confidence band.
* **Method:** Ridge Regression using sentiment-only features to avoid circularity.
* **Performance:** R² = 0.32 (Tech archetype); risk band width ≈ 0.003–0.006.

## 5.4 M4 – Cross-Domain Lag Predictor
* **Goal:** Identify lagged relationships between news domains and portfolio volatility.
* **Method:** VAR models with ADF stationarity tests → Cross-Correlation Function → Granger causality; proven news leads volatility on a 1–3 day horizon.
* **Result:** VAR models confirm news leads volatility on a 1–3 day horizon; significant Granger causality from geopolitics to tech at lags 3–5 days (p < 0.05).

## 5.5 M5 – Sentiment-Volatility Regression
* **Goal:** Quantify how each sentiment domain explains volatility.
* **Method:** Ridge Regression (handles multicollinearity) with domain-specific coefficients.
* **Result:** Geopolitical sentiment explains 73% of variance for the geopolitics portfolio (R² = 0.7321).

## 5.6 M6 – Portfolio Clustering
* **Goal:** Group portfolios based on their sensitivity to news.
* **Method:** K-Means (k = 2, chosen via silhouette score) on M5 coefficient vectors.
* **Outcome:** All four archetypes fall into a single 'Geopolitical-Sensitive' cluster.

# 6. Dashboard
The platform is delivered as a full-stack application — not a simple Streamlit prototype. A FastAPI (Python) backend serves ultra-low-latency inference and metrics, while a React frontend renders the user-facing experience. The system operates in two modes: User Mode for tactical advisory signals, and Technical Mode for model governance and EDA exploration.
* **Backend:** FastAPI (Python) for ultra-low-latency inference and metrics serving.
* **Frontend:** React (high-performance UI) featuring interactive Radar charts, Risk Band visualisations, and a live EDA Research Gallery.
* **Architecture Modes:** Dual-mode architecture including User Mode (tactical Buy/Reduce advice) and Technical Mode (model governance, EDA gallery).
* **Portfolio Features:** Portfolio selection dropdown with shock flag, recovery estimate, and risk band display.

# 7. GenAI Advisory (Groq Integration)
A key differentiator of this platform over conventional dashboards is its integrated GenAI advisory layer. The system does not merely surface numbers — it reasons about them and generates expert-level natural language justifications for every risk signal produced by the ML ensemble.
1. **Model:** Integrated with the Groq API running Llama-3.1-70B for ultra-fast inference.
2. **Value:** For every Buy/Reduce advisory signal emitted by the ensemble, the GenAI layer produces a contextualised, human-readable justification grounded in the current news environment and model outputs — bridging the gap between quantitative signals and investor communication.
3. **Architecture:** The advisory pipeline is fully integrated with the FastAPI backend, ensuring that GenAI responses are served alongside model inference results with minimal additional latency.

# 8. Discussion
The platform demonstrates that news sentiment — especially geopolitical — is a leading indicator of short-term market risk. The Dual-Transformer NLP pipeline, six-model ML ensemble, React + FastAPI dashboard, and Groq-powered advisory layer collectively bridge the gap between raw news metrics and actionable quantitative risk management. Statistical tests highlight the need for a larger historical dataset to achieve conventional significance levels, and the clustering result suggests all current portfolios share a geopolitically-sensitive profile — a finding that may evolve with a broader asset universe.
